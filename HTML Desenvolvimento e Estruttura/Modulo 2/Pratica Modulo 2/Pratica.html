Palmeiras

Palmeiras is a São Paulo team created by members of the Italian community at the beginning of the 20th century, in the Brás neighborhood, in the city of São Paulo. It has state, national and international achievements, such as the Libertadores in 1999, 2020 and 2021. Among the Palmeiras idols, we can mention the five-time champions Cafu and Marcos.

History of Palmeiras

The history of Palmeiras began in the São Paulo neighborhood of Brás, in 1914. At the time, the region was inhabited by Italian communities, who organized themselves to build a sports association that represented them.

The Palestra Itália team, Palmeiras' first name, was born on August 26, 1914, after a meeting between Italians, Brazilians, two Portuguese and a Spaniard, which decided to establish the sports association. Palestra is a term that, translated from Italian, it can mean gymnasium, gym. In general, it refers to the practice of sports.

The team's first game took place on January 24, 1915 against the Savoia team, in the city of Votorantim. This match was 2 x 0 for Palmeiras, and both goals were from penalties.

The first time that Palmeiras, Palestra Itália until then, faced Corinthians was in 1918 and won 3 x 0. Six years after the foundation, in 1920, came the first title, when the team won the Campeonato Paulista.

Palmeiras titles

  • Copa Mercosul: 1998
  • Recopa Sul-Americana: 2022
  • Campeonato Brasileiro: 1960, 1967, 1967 (Taça Brasil), 1969, 1972, 1973, 1993, 1994, 2016, 2018, 2022, 2023
  • Copa do Brasil: 1998, 2012, 2015, 2020
  • Supercopa do Brasil: 2023
  • Copa dos Campeões: 2000
  • Torneio Rio-São Paulo: 1933, 1951, 1965, 1993, 2000
  • Campeonato Paulista: 1920, 1926, 1926 (Extra), 1927, 1932, 1933, 1934, 1936, 1938 (Extra), 1940, 1942, 1944, 1947, 1950, 1959, 1963, 1966, 1972, 1974, 1976, 1993, 1994, 1996, 2008, 2020, 2022, 2023
